Care Tips

  • Water Wisely: Give your plants a drink when the top inch of soil feels dry. Overwatering is just as harmful as underwatering.
  • Choose the Right Light: Make sure to place your plants in appropriate lighting conditions according to their needs. Some need direct sunlight, others thrive in shade.
  • Monitor Humidity: Many houseplants come from humid climates. Use a humidifier or mist your plants regularly to replicate these conditions.
  • Feed Them Well: Fertilize your plants during the growing season for best results, but rest them in fall and winter.
  • Repot When Needed: If your plant has outgrown its pot or the soil is depleted, it's time to repot with fresh soil into a slightly larger container.
  • Avoid Drafts: Keep your plants away from areas with cold drafts or sudden temperature changes to prevent stress.
  • Regular Pruning: Prune dead leaves and stems regularly to encourage new growth and prevent disease.

    • Fertilizer

    • Understand Fertilizer Types: There are various types of fertilizers available - granular, liquid, and slow-release. Choose one that’s best suited for your plant’s needs.
    • Check Nutrient Ratios: Look for the N-P-K ratio on fertilizers, which stands for Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium, and ensure it matches your plant's requirements.
    • Apply Correctly: Apply fertilizers according to the instructions provided. Over-fertilization can damage plant roots and foliage.
    • Seasonal Fertilizing: Fertilize during the growing season when plants are actively growing, usually spring and summer, and reduce during fall and winter.
    • Observe Plant Response: After fertilizing, watch how your plants respond and adjust their fertilization schedule as necessary for optimal growth.
